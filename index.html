TabLauncher Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 3, 2026

Developer: olekkdev · olekkdev@proton.me

The Short Version

TabLauncher is an Android home screen launcher. Almost everything happens on your device and stays on your device. The only data sent off-device is anonymous crash reports (Firebase Crashlytics) and anonymous usage analytics (Firebase Analytics). Both are opt-out in the app settings.

Data That Stays on Your Device

The following data is stored and processed locally only and is never sent to any server:

  • Installed app list (package names and labels) — displayed in the launcher drawer; a subset is stored in a local database for tab and category assignments
  • Settings (clock format, layout preferences, theme) — stored in Proto DataStore on-device
  • Organization data (tabs, categories, pinned apps) — stored in a local Room database
  • App icons — held in memory only, never written to disk
  • System wallpaper — read to render the app drawer background with a dark overlay, held in memory only
  • Time and date — displayed on the home screen, never stored or transmitted

Backup & Export

Manual backup: You can export your settings and organization data as a JSON file using the in-app backup feature. The file is saved to a location you choose via the system file picker. It contains tabs, categories, pinned apps, and settings. It does not include wallpaper images, app icons, or any personal content.

Android Auto Backup: Your settings and database may be backed up to your Google Drive through standard Android backup, operated by Google LLC (Google Privacy Policy). This is controlled by your device's backup settings and your Google account—not by TabLauncher. Legal basis: your consent via device backup opt-in.

Data Sent Off-Device

Crash Reports — Firebase Crashlytics

TabLauncher uses Firebase Crashlytics (operated by Google LLC) to collect anonymous crash reports. When the app crashes, the following is sent:

  • Crash stack traces
  • Crashlytics installation UUID (anonymous, per-install, reset on reinstall)
  • Device model, OS version, orientation
  • RAM, disk space
  • Whether the device is rooted
  • App version name and code
  • Crash timestamp
  • Device architecture (CPU type)

Purpose: Identify and fix bugs that cause crashes, improving app stability.

Legal basis: Legitimate interest in maintaining a stable, reliable app.

Encryption: All crash data is encrypted in transit using TLS.

Retention: Crash data is retained for 90 days. Aggregated crash-free statistics may be retained longer.

Opt out: You can disable crash reporting in Settings → About → Send Crash Reports.

Usage Analytics — Firebase Analytics

TabLauncher uses Firebase Analytics (operated by Google LLC) to collect anonymous usage data. The following is sent:

  • App opens and session duration
  • Firebase installation ID (anonymous, per-install, reset on reinstall)
  • Device model, OS version, language
  • Country or region (derived from IP address; IP is not stored)
  • App version name and code

Purpose: Understand how the app is used in aggregate to guide product decisions.

Legal basis: Legitimate interest in improving the app experience.

Encryption: All analytics data is encrypted in transit using TLS.

Retention: Event data is retained for 2 months by default. Aggregated reports may be retained longer.

Opt out: You can disable analytics in Settings → About → Send Analytics.

Third-party privacy policies: Firebase Privacy · Google Privacy Policy

What We Do NOT Collect

TabLauncher does not collect, store, or transmit:

  • Names, email addresses, or account information
  • Location data
  • Photos, contacts, or messages
  • Browsing history
  • Advertising IDs

Your data is never sold and is never used for advertising.

App Permissions Explained

Launchers need a few permissions that may look broad. Here is exactly what each one does and why:

  • QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES Lists the apps installed on your device so they can appear in the launcher drawer. Required for any launcher to function.
  • REQUEST_DELETE_PACKAGES Lets you trigger an app uninstall from the launcher. The system still shows a confirmation dialog—nothing is removed without your approval.
  • SET_WALLPAPER Sets a new wallpaper when you pick a custom background image in settings.
  • READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE / READ_MEDIA_IMAGES Reads your current system wallpaper bitmap purely in memory to render the app drawer background. The image is never stored or transmitted. On Android 13+, the narrower READ_MEDIA_IMAGES permission is used instead.

International Data Transfer

Firebase Crashlytics and Firebase Analytics process data on servers in the United States. These transfers are covered by Google's Standard Contractual Clauses and their data processing terms. No other data leaves your device.

Children's Privacy

TabLauncher is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided data through crash reporting, contact us at olekkdev@proton.me and we will take steps to delete it.

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Access the data associated with your device
  • Request deletion of crash data linked to your installation
  • Object to crash or analytics data collection (disable either in-app under Settings → About, or contact us)

To exercise any of these rights, email olekkdev@proton.me.

California Residents (CCPA)

TabLauncher does not sell personal information to any third party. Anonymous crash reports and analytics sent to Firebase are used solely for bug fixing and product improvement and are not shared for advertising or marketing purposes.
